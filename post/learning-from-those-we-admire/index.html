Learning From Those We Admire

Mon, Feb 14, 2022

You should interview people you admire. They have stories and experiences that you can learn from. Others have given this recommendation as well. Yet, taking that admiration and turning it into a productive conversation is difficult. Here are several tips on how to get the most out of those conversations.

Start with what you admire

Start with what you admired and turn it into a question. For example, if they excelled at handling a major outage, ask “How did you become good at handling major outages.” There is usually a situation, a skill, or some previous experience they have. Find it and use it to get the conversation going.

Move into other areas

Then, plan to move into another area. The skill or situation had a lifetime of work behind it so learn more about that base experiences. Ask questions tied to areas you want to grow in or questions where the person specializes.

Sometimes it’s not clear what those areas are so general questions can still help. But, “tell me about your career”, is a broad question. Try some of the questions below and improvise on them.

  • “What has surprised you during your career?”
  • “If you could change one thing what would it be and why?”
  • “What is something you think people in our industry should talk about that we don’t"?

Roll with the unplanned

Remember it’s a conversation. If things go sideways, roll with it. I had some of my best conversations because we segued into something random. It’s good to have questions prepared but the goal is to learn from the person. It’s better to learn from them than to stick with the questions you wrote ahead of time.
